Taploop

Taploop was designed as an iOS app for sharing experience about game. I expect there can be some ideas about how to play a game, or some ideas about how to creative a game, hopely not just video game, but also generalized concept of game, like how to play an RPG in real world. Well, that is the hope.

- part 2 -

The feeds and the algorithm

Part 2 is still a design show but also not quite. Yes, the basic style of the cards for different types of articles, like text mainly, video mainly, image mainly, or the editor's pick, was designed by me, but it is also planted a kind of algorithm in the home page designed by our genius algorithm engineer Zhao Lin Jun. In her design, the recommendation algorithm here is not for suggesting people to read something he/she might like, but for suggesting people to read something we like. Of course the first step must be suggesting something related to his/her interest, but correlation of the feeds shall be decreasing. Once the user closed the app or just stop scrolling the feed, we will know the "border" of his/her interest.

This is completely out of my job, but the reason I like this idea is simple. The hot games in China might not be as good as their fame. Some real good games like Banner Saga, Braids or Apotheon, etc., are relatively unfamous. We want to guide people to these kind of games, in a UGC platform.

- part 5 -

3 steps to get your surprise

It is called surprise, actually it is inspired by TED Talk. Sometime, you don't even know what you want to read, so, we give you a change to randomly pick one. Well, if you use TED, you are already familiar with that I believe.

The step 1 is completely the same as TED. You choose the time you have, then go up next.

The design of step 2 is demanded by Zhao Linjun, the algorithm engineer I mentioned before. She loves cats, so she insisted to we should use cats to express emotions. So, in step 2, you will pick the emotion you might be in now, relax, anxious, boring, sad, and up to step 3.
